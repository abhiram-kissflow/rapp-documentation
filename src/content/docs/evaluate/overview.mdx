RAPP is an AI-powered app generation platform. Instead of writing code, designing databases, or dragging widgets around a canvas, you **describe the app you need in plain English** — by typing or speaking — and RAPP builds it for you.

Think of it as having a senior developer on call who interviews you about your requirements and then produces a complete application specification in minutes.

## The problem RAPP solves

Building business applications today is slow and expensive. Even with low-code tools, teams spend weeks configuring workflows, designing forms, and defining business rules. Requirements get lost in translation between business stakeholders and developers. The result: long timelines, scope creep, and apps that don't match what was originally envisioned.

RAPP eliminates that gap. The person who understands the business need — a team lead, product manager, or operations head — describes what they want directly to an AI that understands business applications. No intermediaries, no specification documents to write by hand, no drag-and-drop learning curves.

## How it works

The user journey follows four stages:

1. **Describe** — You land on RAPP and type or speak your app idea. For example: "I need a leave management system where employees submit requests and managers approve them."

2. **Interview** — The AI asks 3 to 5 targeted follow-up questions. It already infers roughly 80% of your requirements from your initial description and only asks about the remaining gaps — approval hierarchies, notification preferences, special rules.

3. **Blueprint** — RAPP generates a complete application specification: an overview, user roles, step-by-step processes, data forms, business rules, and integration points. This is your app's blueprint.

4. **Build** — The blueprint opens in the visual Builder, where you can review every section, refine details through AI-assisted chat, and preview the generated application.

<Aside type="tip">
Already have a requirements document? Upload it (PDF, Word, or text) and RAPP extracts the specification automatically — no interview needed.
</Aside>

## Who is RAPP for?

RAPP is designed for teams that need internal business applications — the kind of tools that every organization builds over and over:

- **Leave and attendance management**
- **Expense reporting and approvals**
- **Applicant tracking and onboarding**
- **Inventory and asset management**
- **Customer request portals**
- **Project tracking dashboards**

The primary users are business stakeholders — product leads, operations managers, department heads — who understand what they need but don't want to learn a development tool to get it. Developers benefit too: RAPP produces structured specifications that are easy to review, extend, and implement.

## Key value propositions

**Conversational input.** No forms to fill, no canvases to learn. Just describe what you need in your own words, by text or voice.

**AI at the core.** AI is not a bolt-on feature. It powers the entire workflow: understanding your intent, asking smart questions, generating complete specifications, and assisting with refinement.

**Minutes, not weeks.** Go from an idea to a detailed application blueprint in a single session. The AI handles the heavy lifting of structuring requirements.

**Complete output.** The generated blueprint covers roles, processes, forms, business rules, and integrations — everything needed to build or review an application.

<Aside>
RAPP is under active development. The platform currently generates detailed specifications (blueprints). Full application code generation is on the roadmap.
</Aside>
